Reflection

THIS IS A DRAFT

AI is a fresh field for everyone. And venturing into the unknown can sometimes be frustrating, and rightly so! Mastering new skills is always challenging, especially in a field that is so new to the world. AI not only provides value to a person's daily work flow, but they also can impact and aid in almost anything.

This semester, I had the opportunity to interview five people with varying levels of experience with AI: Bella, with experience working with AI in her daily workflow, Anthony, a ChatGPT connoisseur, Francesca, a complete novice to everything AI, Michael, a on-and-off AI user, and Alexa, someone that has used it before but not in-the-know with technology. I learned from their perspectives how AI affects their daily lives, why they continue to be users' and what they see for the future of AI.

The specifics of what I learned from each individual are located in their articles (User Interviews), now, I am focused on reflecting on the experience of broadening my perspective on AI by looking at others' perspectives on AI. As a product designer, I do not shy away from AI and it has only positively impacted my daily workflow. Whether that be creating lists and ideas for me on a less-creative day, or a programming question when my professor is unavailable. There are various avenues or situations in which I have chosen to use AI and, sometimes, there are issues, but without issues, you would not learn how to properly debug and understand what to say and what not to say to get an output that satisfies your input.

Speaking to people who have different experiences with AI has made me realize that AI can serve and help with so many things and it is not a mutually exclusive tool to any field of study. There is an aspect of having some 'artistic freedom' when you open that chatbot and all that is there is a text box for you to write whatever comes to mind. Also, something I knew before, but have gotten a deeper understanding of throughout this process is the ability to talk to a chatbot effectively and the vernacular required to get the output you are expecting.

If it wasn't for both my coworkers and the influx of the use of AI lately, I wouldn't have done this topic. So I truly have tothank the zeitgeist for bringing AI to the forefront and creating a space for those, like me, to play with it and even learn how to talk effectively to it. When I finally felt I had talking to a chatbot effectively mastered, I loved being able to help others with learning how to use AI!
